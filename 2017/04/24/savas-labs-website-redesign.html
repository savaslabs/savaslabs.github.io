Although we‚Äôre usually building client websites in Drupal, we chose to continue with the static site generator Jekyll which we‚Äôve written about before as our platform. Ultimately, for us, the benefits outweighed the drawbacks:

Bennies

  1. No database back-end, meaning reduced security threats and reduced hosting complexity. We‚Äôre able to host for free on GitHub Pages.
  2. A highly-performant framework.
  3. Ease of deployment.
  4. Efficient for our workflow as it is developer oriented.
  5. Inertia.

Drawbacks

  1. Requires Ruby ecosystem competency which often presents the need to acquire new skills for Drupalers.
  2. Requires developer-level skills to update.
  3. Not as robust of an ecosystem as Drupal for example.
    1. Example: When we were dissatisfied with our comment options, we built our own server on guess what, a different framework. This took us time and is less feature-rich than what we‚Äôd get with Drupal out of the box.

These drawbacks, though not without their costs, are also opportunities for us to be a part of the broader web ecosystem and learn. The more exposure to best-practices and other frameworks, the more we can borrow from and employ them in our work, though it is an investment to develop the expertise.

Jekyll works for us since the team of savvy Savasians is comfortable rapidly learning a new workflow to publish updates to our website. Additionally our website needs are relatively simple, certainly compared to the needs of many of our partners. Our partners often have personnel with a wide range of technical training and often require sophisticated content models, granular authentication and permissions, advanced content publishing workflows among many other common needs for which a modular ecosystem of plugins like Drupal‚Äôs would be better suited.

Alongside Jekyll, we used Hologram developed by Trulia for our style guide. It proved to be a great workflow for our designer, front-end developer and myself to interact efficiently and see updated results in the browser rapidly.
